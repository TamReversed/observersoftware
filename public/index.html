Observer exists because its founders noticed people working around technology instead of with it.

The work starts with watching. People take shortcuts for a reason. They keep shadow notes. They re-enter data. They ask a colleague because the system won’t answer. Those patterns are the requirements.

Many tools fail by treating workflows as diagrams instead of lived routines. Observer prefers restraint: fewer screens, fewer decisions, fewer moving parts. Keep the path clear. Make state obvious. Leave the rest out.
